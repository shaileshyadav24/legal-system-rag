Kubernetes assembles one or more computers, either virtual machines or bare metal, into a cluster which can run workloads in containers. It works with various container runtimes, such as containerd and CRI-O.[6] Its suitability for running and managing workloads of all sizes and styles has led to its widespread adoption in clouds and data centers.
